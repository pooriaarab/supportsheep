Mirrored 5% of reads — what migrating off Postgres to PlanetScale actually taught us

by Pooria Arab · 8 min read · drafted from interview · 19 May 2026

Our Postgres setup was healthy — until it wasn't. A handful of long-running transactions during EU peak hours started knocking over read replicas, and the fix-vs-replace conversation had been quietly looping for two quarters. PlanetScale promised horizontal scale without the operational weight, but the team's gut said: don't trust the promise; trust the migration plan.

The mirror-first approach

We didn't cut over. We mirrored: roughly 5% of read traffic to PlanetScale in parallel with Postgres for two weeks. The point wasn't performance benchmarks — it was discovering the shape of failure. What surprised us was that error rate on PlanetScale was meaningfully lower than on Postgres. Embarrassing, useful.

Writes were the harder problem. We dual-wrote for ten days, then reconciled with an hourly checksum job. Two rows ever fell out of sync — and both turned out to be a timezone bug in the checksum itself, not the databases.

"Mirror reads for longer than you think you need to. The hour you'd save by rushing is bought back 100x by the bugs you don't ship."
— Pooria Arab

What we'd do differently

Two things. First, we'd dual-write for fifteen days, not ten — the timezone bug we caught at day eight wouldn't have surfaced otherwise…
